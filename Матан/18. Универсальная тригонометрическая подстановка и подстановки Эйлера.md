## Универсальная тригонометрическая подстановка
$$ \Large  
\int R(\cos x,\sin x)\,dx  
$$  
  
$$ \Large  
\operatorname{tg}\frac{x}{2}=t  \quad \operatorname{tg}^2\frac{x}{2}=t^2  
$$
  
$$ \Large  
\operatorname{tg}^2\frac{x}{2}+1  
=  
\frac{\sin^2 \frac{x}{2}}{\cos^2 \frac{x}{2}}+1  
=  
\frac{1}{\cos^2 \frac{x}{2}}  
$$  
  
$$ \Large  
\cos^2 \frac{x}{2}=\frac{1}{1+t^2}  
$$  
  
$$ \Large  
\cos x = 2\cos^2 \frac{x}{2}-1  
=  
\frac{2}{1+t^2}-1  
=  
\frac{1-t^2}{1+t^2}  
$$  
  
$$ \Large  
\sin x = 2\sin \frac{x}{2}\cos \frac{x}{2}  
=  
2\cdot \frac{\sin \frac{x}{2}}{\cos \frac{x}{2}} \cdot \cos^2 \frac{x}{2}  
=  
\frac{2t}{1+t^2}  
$$  
  
$$ \Large  
\frac{x}{2}=\operatorname{arctg} t + C  
$$  
  
$$ \Large  
x'(t)=\frac{2}{1+t^2}  
$$  
  
$$ \Large  
\int R(\cos x,\sin x)\,dx  
=  
2\int R\left(\frac{1-t^2}{1+t^2},\frac{2t}{1+t^2}\right)\cdot \frac{1}{1+t^2}\,dt  
$$
