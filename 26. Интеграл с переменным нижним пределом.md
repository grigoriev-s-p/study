## Определение

$$ \Large
f \in R([a,b]), \quad x \in [a,b)
$$

$$ \Large
\Psi(x) = \int\limits_x^b f(y)\,dy \quad (18)
$$

$$ \Large
\Psi(b) \overset{\text{def}}{=} 0 \quad (19)
$$

---

## Теорема

$$ \Large
f \in R([a,b]) \Rightarrow \Psi(x) \in C([a,b]) \quad (1)
$$

$$ \Large
\forall x_0 \in [a,b]: \quad f \text{ непр. в } x_0
\Rightarrow \Psi'(x_0) = -f(x_0) \quad (2)
$$

### Доказательство
#### Докажем (1)

$$ \Large
\Phi(x) + \Psi(x)
=
\int\limits_a^x f(y)\,dy + \int\limits_x^b f(y)\,dy
=
\int\limits_a^b f(y)\,dy = C_0  \Leftrightarrow
$$

$$ \Large
 \Leftrightarrow \Psi(x) = C_0 - \Phi(x), \quad x \in [a,b] 
$$

$$ \Large
\Rightarrow (1)
$$
#### Докажем (2)
$$ \Large
\exists\Psi'(x_0)
=
(C_0 - \Phi(x))'
=
0 - \Phi'(x_0)
=
- f(x_0)
\quad \Rightarrow (2)
$$
